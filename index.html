Dhyey M. Shah

MSE Robotics @ University of Pennsylvania • Previously: ModalAI / IIT Gandhinagar

learn ⇄ design ⇆ build

I’m Dhyey Shah, a second-year Master’s student in Robotics (MSE) at the University of Pennsylvania, where I am advised by Prof. Nadia Figueroa.

I am broadly interested in building end-to-end robotic systems, with a focus on novel control frameworks for human-centric robotic environments. More recently, I have been fascinated by reinforcement learning–based control strategies for applications such as robotic manipulation and aerial robotics.

At the GRASP Lab, I currently work with the Figueroa Robotics Lab on the development of an upper-limb exoskeleton for assistive and resistive training in collaborative human–robot environments. Previously, I worked with the Kod*Lab on template-based control methods for legged robots. You can find more details about my research and publications on my Research page.

Beyond research, I have gained industry experience as a Software Development Intern at ModalAI, where I worked on real-time image processing and vision-based control pipelines for UAVs. I have also served as the SLAM lead for a social indoor robot developed for the second round of the SICK $10K LiDAR Challenge. A complete overview of my technical and course projects can be found on my Projects page.

Outside the lab, I enjoy spending time around people as much as robots. I actively play and follow sports such as cricket, tennis, badminton, and cycling, and have recently been exploring rock climbing. I also enjoy hiking with friends, painting, reading, and writing—often journaling or expressing thoughts through poetry.

I have a long-standing curiosity about psychology, philosophy, and neuroscience, particularly in understanding human behavior and cognition. Growing up in Vadodara, Gujarat, I fluently speak English, Hindi, and Gujarati, and am currently learning Spanish.
